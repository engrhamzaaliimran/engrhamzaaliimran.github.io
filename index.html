About

I received my BSc in Electrical Engineering from NUCES-FAST (2018) and an MSc in Computer Science from NUST-SEECS (2022). I am currently pursuing my PhD at Saarland University, where my research focuses on Metal-Oxide Semiconductor (MOS) gas sensors and Electrical Impedance Spectroscopy (EIS). My work investigates frequency-domain sensing, model-based impedance analysis, and sparse-frequency excitation schemes to enable robust and deployable gas-sensing systems. Prior to my PhD, I accumulated five and a half years of industry experience at Emumba (2018–2024), working across embedded systems, QA automation, and Generative AI, with a strong emphasis on system-level development and deployment. This AIoT-oriented background now directly informs my research, particularly in translating measurement-driven sensor insights into compact, edge-ready sensing pipelines. I also previously served as a Research Assistant at the EPIC Lab (2017–2018), where I gained early research experience in Internet of Things and Embedded Systems.

Selected Research & AIoT Projects

AIoT-Enabled Sleep Monitoring with Actigraphy

Developed lightweight deep learning models for large-scale sleep–wake detection using wrist-worn inertial sensors on the MESA cohort. Designed edge-deployable architectures combining temporal modeling and attention mechanisms for long-term wearable health monitoring.

Keywords: Actigraphy · Edge AI · Transformers · Health AI · Wearables

AIoT-Based Parkinson’s Symptom Intelligence

Designed deep transfer learning pipelines for real-world Parkinson’s motor symptom assessment using wearable inertial sensors. Leveraged pretrained human activity models and Explainable AI (XAI) to analyze progression from healthy to Parkinsonian motion patterns.

Keywords: Inertial Sensors · Edge AI · XAI · Transfer Learning · Digital Health

Cross-Domain Emotion Recognition from Gait (IEEE IoT Journal)

Proposed cross-domain transfer learning for emotion recognition using wearable motion sensors. Demonstrated that generalizable human movement priors learned from large activity datasets significantly improve data-scarce affect recognition tasks while remaining lightweight for mobile deployment.

View Publication

Keywords: Wearable AI · Transfer Learning · Edge Intelligence · Human Motion

BLE Mesh Environmental Monitoring System

Built an ESP32-based BLE mesh architecture for distributed environmental sensing with Android integration. Designed low-power communication pipelines for scalable IoT deployment.

Keywords: ESP32 · BLE Mesh · Embedded Systems · IoT Networks
